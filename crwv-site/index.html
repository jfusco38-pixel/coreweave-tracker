Lynch pitch — why this could work, in plain English

Bull case Lynch

"What does the company do? Why might this stock work?"

  • One sentence: CRWV runs a GPU-cloud business that rents NVIDIA-based AI compute to hyperscalers and AI labs.
  • Why it could work: Demand visibility is unusually high for a young company. RPO grew from $15.1B to $60.7B in one year — that's contracted future revenue customers have already promised.
    As of December 31, 2025, we had $60.7 billion of remaining performance obligations ("RPO"), compared to $15.1 … CRWV 10-K (FY25), p.1 / Item 1 Business overview
  • How it makes money: Multi-year compute contracts. Recent ones include Meta ($14.2B through Dec 2031) and OpenAI ($11.9B through 2031).
    Meta initially committed to pay us up to approximately $14.2 billion through December 2031 … CRWV 10-K, Risk Factors / Customer concentration
    OpenAI has committed to pay us up to approximately $11.9 billion through 2031 … CRWV 10-K, Item 1A Risk Factors
  • Revenue scale-up is real: $5.1B in 2025 vs $1.9B in 2024 vs $0.23B in 2023. Triple-digit growth at multi-billion-dollar scale is rare.
    Revenue for the year ended December 31, 2025 increased by $3.2 billion, or 168%, compared to the year ended December 31, 2024. CRWV 10-K, MD&A · Revenue
  • Why the market might be missing it: The headline net loss of $1.2B and $21.4B of debt look scary. If the OpenAI / Meta / Microsoft contracts convert RPO to recognized revenue on schedule, GAAP losses could compress fast as the data-center capex gets matched by depreciation rather than upfront cash burn.

Bear case Munger invert

"How could I lose money? What would prove the bear case right?"

  • Customer concentration is extreme. One customer is two-thirds of the revenue. If Microsoft renegotiates, slows take-up, or insources, the entire model breaks.
    We recognized an aggregate of approximately 67% of our revenue from our top customer, Microsoft, for the year ended December 31, 2025. CRWV 10-K, Risk Factors
  • The balance sheet is the bet. $21.4B of total debt against $3.1B of cash and a $1.2B net loss. Operating profitability flipped negative in 2025 even though revenue tripled.
    Operating income (loss): (46) [2025] · 324 [2024] · (14) [2023]  (in $M) CRWV 10-K, Consolidated Statements of Operations
  • Stock-based compensation just exploded. SBC was $630M in FY25 vs $31M in FY24 — mostly IPO-related but materially dilutive.
    Total stock-based compensation expense: $630M [2025] · $31M [2024] · $15M [2023] CRWV 10-K, Note: Stock-Based Compensation
  • Cumulative losses keep widening. $(594)M, $(863)M, then $(1,167)M three years running.
    We incurred net losses of $1.2 billion, $863 million, and $594 million for the years ended December 31, 2025, 2024, and 2023 CRWV 10-K, Risk Factors / Going-concern context
  • What would prove me wrong (i.e., the bear right): Microsoft renegotiates terms downward, OpenAI/Meta delivery slips, the next debt raise prices at a punitive coupon, or Hyperscaler self-build programs (Stargate, Microsoft AI infra) start absorbing what would have been CRWV demand.

What I'd watch next quarter

  • Customer A %: Did Microsoft drop below 60% as OpenAI/Meta scale? That's the de-concentration signal.
  • RPO conversion: Quarterly revenue / opening RPO ratio. Tells you whether the $60.7B backlog is delivering on the promised cadence.
  • Operating margin trajectory: Is the FY25 swing to a (46)M operating loss reversing as utilization improves, or is it widening?
  • Net debt: Did the ratio of debt to RPO move in the right direction? $21.4B debt against $60.7B RPO is 35% — the number to track.
  • Capex / power siting: Any commentary on data-center power availability or new site delays. CRWV's growth is gated by gigawatts, not chips.
